  • 1- Plan meals ahead of time (plan a menu AND shop to have ingredients on hand).
  • 2- Simple recipes with few ingredients:

    a- Try simple pastas, stir-fries or one-pot dishes (check out the recipes attached – they’re quick and simple)!
    b- You can try more complicated recipes when you have more time and as you gain confidence.
  • 3- Add plenty of fresh fruit and vegetables:

    a- Add fruit to breakfast cereal.
    b- Have tinned baked beans (low-salt/no added salt varieties) on toast.
    c- Wash and roughly cut up carrots, celery and snow peas so they are ready to eat when you are in a hurry. Most fruit just needs to be washed!

Check your pantry, freezer and fridge. Stock these with basic items (Newsletter 2), which form the base of almost any meal. This makes cooking easy and quick.

Be a super shopper. Write a shopping list and take it with you! Buy fruit and vegetables in season or tinned and frozen when they are not.

Freeze correctly. Freezing food that you won’t use straight away will save you money and time! Check Newsletter 4 to see how long different foods freeze for.

Keep food safe. Think safety when preparing and storing your food to avoid contamination and waste.
